The challenge

Healthcare data is fragmented, disconnected, and insecure.

Fragmented records

Patient history is split across paper, siloed facilities, and hospital-specific systems.

Unreliable connectivity

Power and network instability mean offline-first design is essential, not optional.

Unequal access

Different actors need different channels: QR codes, USSD, Android apps, and web dashboards.

Governance risk

Consent, audit, provenance, and minimum-necessary disclosure must be built into every workflow.

Product

MedCore is built around an interoperable, channel-aware core.

Multichannel delivery

Support for QR, USSD, Android, and Web lets patients and practitioners interact through the right channel for their context.

FHIR-aligned data model

All clinical and workflow data maps to a shared FHIR R4 canonical model to enable portability, integration, and future growth.

Offline-first sync

Local data capture with conflict-safe sync preserves continuity in areas with poor connectivity.

Governance by design

Every action is audited, consent is surfaced in workflows, and access decisions are enforced with role-based controls.

Architecture

Modular architecture for resilient care delivery and secure interoperability.

MedCore’s technical design centers on a FastAPI backend, FHIR-aligned storage, and a modular service structure that supports patient apps, provider dashboards, file storage, and immutable audit logging.

  • Patient app and provider dashboard connect through a shared API.
  • FHIR-styled resources power patient profiles, encounters, medications, lab results, and documents.
  • Audit logs record every access, edit, and share event for compliance.
  • File storage holds attachments, scans, and records with secure access links.

Research

Offline-first resilience
